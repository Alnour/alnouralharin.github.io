Reinforcement learning (RL) has achieved remarkable success in various domains, from playing complex games to robotic control. However, understanding *why* an RL agent makes certain decisions remains a significant challenge.

## The Interpretation Challenge

As RL systems are deployed in increasingly critical applications — healthcare, autonomous vehicles, financial trading — the need for interpretability becomes paramount. Stakeholders need to understand not just *what* an agent does, but *why* it does it.

## Key Approaches

Our survey categorizes interpretation methods into several families:

1. **Saliency Maps** — Highlighting which parts of the input most influence the agent's decisions
2. **Policy Distillation** — Compressing complex policies into simpler, more interpretable models
3. **Reward Decomposition** — Breaking down the reward signal to understand what drives behavior
4. **Trajectory Analysis** — Examining sequences of states and actions to identify patterns

## Why It Matters

The gap between performance and understanding is one of the most important challenges in modern AI. As we build more capable systems, our ability to interpret and verify their behavior becomes a critical safety requirement.
